What is UX Design?

User experience design, commonly known as UX design, is the process of creating a meaningful and relevant product centered around the needs and desires of a target audience. In other words, UX design is designing how a user interacts with a product.

The role of a UX designer involves research. They must understand how their target audience thinks and feels, but also how certain features can elicit specific reactions from them. UX design isn’t just some one-time solution; it is an on-going process that involves continuously gathering data and user-feedback to ensure that the product continues to satisfy expectations as a user’s needs evolve.

UX designers must advocate for their users’ needs throughout all stages of the design process. They must understand their audience well to develop the best solutions to address their habits, needs, and concerns.

What is UI Design?

User interface design, also known as UI design, focuses on the aesthetic and functional experience of consumers. In other words, they create the look and feel of an interface. It is the job of the UI designer to bring the UX designer’s vision to life to ensure the creation of a product that is both easy and pleasing to use.

UI designers oversee the colors, text, font, spacing, images, buttons, and all interactive elements of a site. Therefore, UI designers must have a strong understanding of design principles to represent concepts and ideas in a way that is easy for the target audience to understand. They must work with the framework of the site to develop a design that is functional, attractive, and aligned with all brand elements on the page.

Why is UX/UI Design Important?

By understanding what drives human behavior, UX and UI designers can design websites and applications that evoke positive experiences and responses from users. Studies have shown that good UX and UI design not only improve a consumer’s overall experience, but also increases user sales, their probability to return (and become loyal consumers) and brand equity. In fact, studies show that for every $1 invested into UX design, business on average receive $100 in return.

Businesses that keep consumers in the center of the design process experience the most satisfaction and returns than those who don’t.

What makes good UX/UI Design?

While UX and UI design are different, together the ensure the creation of a product that enables users to access information or complete tasks in an efficient and pleasing way. You cannot have good UX without good UI.

Good Structure

A well-structured site ensures that users can find everything they need and that the overall experiences feel natural. It will also be more accessible for individuals with disabilities.

When first structuring you site, it is important to keep your business or brand’s future goals in mind. The first structure of your site should be able to easily adapt to expand to meet your goals.

Communicative

Users should feel represented when they load your site. Let them know that you heard their thoughts, ideas, and concerns and took them into consideration when designing the product.

Continuously gather user feedback to ensure that your site remains up-to-date and appealing to your target audience. Depending on the purpose of your site, perhaps a comment section, or contact page could help users engage with content more and feel heard.

Concise

Users should be able to understand what your page is about without any difficulty. From navigating through content, to reading copy, the user experience should be intuitive.

UX and UI design does not just take into consideration the artistic design and layout of a site, but the words as well. Buttons and navigation links should tell users exactly what they do in as little words as possible. Everything on the site should be useable and useful, there shouldn’t be any excess content that isn’t necessary.

Visually Appealing

When your site first loads onto a user’s browser, they should be enticed to stay on the page and explore its contents. To ensure this happens, make sure the website’s aesthetics appeal to the them.

It is important to understand what appeals to your target audience design wise – listen to their concerns and ideas gathered from research to ensure the product is something they desire. The color palette should resonate well with them, as well as the images used. Typography should be legible and not be formatted in large blocks of text.

Steps of the Design Process

  1. Research
    • The first step of the design process is gathering data, which can be either qualitative, quantitative, or both. Research allows designers to understand their target audience more and determining the issues they have and the solutions they are seeking
  2. Design
    • In this stage, designers begin to brainstorm potential solutions for users. This may involve sketching ideas, toying with colors, changing the interface and structure of the site.
  3. Prototype
    • This is where you create a more realistic mock-up of the final product that enables you to simulate how consumers will feel. The purpose of this stage is to test what you have already to resolve and fine-tune any issues before more time and money are spent into developing it.
